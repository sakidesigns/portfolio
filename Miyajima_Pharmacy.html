Project

Miyajima Pharmacy

Responsive Website
View Live Site

Overview

This responsive pharmacy website is designed to provide a seamless user experience for customers seeking pharmaceutical services across three different locations in Yamaguchi, Japan. My client, the owner of the pharmacy, aims to showcase the range of services offered, including drive-through access, cashless payment options, and the ability to submit prescriptions through LINE Messenger.

The Problem Statement


  • Users struggle to access complete pharmacy, location, and recruitment info on the current website.

  • Inconsistent user experiences across devices due to lack of responsive design.

  • Limited visibility of key services like drive-through and LINE Messenger prescriptions.

The Solution Statement


  • Implement responsive design for seamless user experiences.

  • Enhance navigation for easy access to pharmacy services and recruitment info.

  • Highlight key services like drive-through and LINE Messenger prescriptions for better user engagement.

Ideate

During the ideate phase for the Miyajima Pharmacy website, I gathered feedback and conducted competitor analysis to generate ideas for enhancing the user experience.

I like


  • I like how the primary service is prominently displayed at the top of the page.

  • I like how each pharmacy location is clearly listed with its respective operating hours, ensuring users can easily access the information they need.

I wish


  • I wish for interactive elements such as chat support or FAQs to assist users in understanding and utilizing services effectively.

  • I wish for a user-friendly interface that intuitively guides visitors to essential information about pharmaceutical services and location details..

What if


  • What if we create virtual tours or interactive guides to familiarize new customers with the pharmacy's layout and services before their visit?

  • What if we partner with local delivery services to offer convenient home delivery options for prescriptions and healthcare products?

  • What if we integrate real-time updates on medication availability and stock levels to inform users and prevent disappointment when visiting the pharmacy?

I conducted a competitor analysis of three local pharmacies: Pelican Pharmacy, Tsubasa Pharmacy, and Sogo Pharmacy. This analysis aimed to identify strengths and weaknesses that could inform the redesign and improve the overall user experience.
Pelican Pharmacy
Tsubasa Pharmacy
Sogo Pharmacy
Overview

Pelican Pharmacy, based in Ube City, Yamaguchi, Japan, is a comprehensive healthcare provider with five branches: four in Yamaguchi and one in Shimane Prefecture. This regional presence ensures accessibility to a wider population for their healthcare services.

Based in Ube City, Tsubasa Pharmacy caters to the needs of Yamaguchi residents by offering convenient services such as cashless transactions and 24-hour prescription acceptance via FAX. Additionally, they provide an outdoor prescription reception window for infection prevention measures.

Sogo Pharmacy, headquartered in Tokyo with branches in Ube and a strong presence, offers a range of services including online medication guidance, cashless transactions, home healthcare, and health consultations.
Strengths

  • Clean design and easy navigation

  • Visually appealing with high-quality images

  • Adapts well to different screen sizes

  • Maps for branch locations included

  • Comprehensive services offered

  • Services and contact details provided

  • Easily accessible contact details

  • Professional and trustworthy appearance

  • Instills trust and credibility with a polished appearance

  • Utilizes the search bar effectively for locating different branches or products based on location.

  • Enhances visual appeal and aids in quick recognition of feature with icons

Weakness

  • Business hours not listed on the landing page, which may be important information for visitors.

  • Branch information not easily accessible in the nav bar.

  • Mobile responsiveness needs improvement

  • Lack of localization options

  • Limited interactive features

  • Potential for more engaging visual elements

  • While the website provides basic information about products and services, it lacks in-depth content or resources that could further educate and engage visitors.

  • Given that Sogo Pharmacy has branches in Ube, the website could benefit from localized content or information tailored to users in that area, such as promotions or events specific to the Ube branch.

Design

Creating high-fidelity wireframes, I arranged key components based on user needs, utilizing plenty of white space for a minimalist, clean design inspired by competitor websites. This inclusion of minimalist white space greatly improved the website's visual hierarchy, making it ideal for pharmacy sites with its clean look.

The user flow for Miyajima Pharmacy's website was crafted to offer visitors a seamless and intuitive experience. I mapped out user journeys, identifying entry points, interactions, and pathways for efficient access to information and services on the site.

The Miyajima Pharmacy website's information architecture includes a landing page with a services summary, details about each branch, and a prominent recruitment banner. This setup allows users to access key information easily and navigate through sections efficiently, including recruitment opportunities.

a grid system was implemented to ensure consistent and responsive layout across different devices. The phone grid features 5 columns with a 20px gutter and 25px margin, optimizing content placement and spacing for smaller screens. On the laptop version, a 10-column grid with an 85px margin and 45px gutter was used to accommodate larger displays

The logo I designed for the Miyajima pharmacy is symbolic and visually represents the essence of the pharmacy. Here's an explanation of the design elements.


Theme


  • Two Pills Crossed Together: This symbolizes the core focus of the pharmacy on healthcare and medication. It immediately conveys to customers that the pharmacy is a place for medical solutions and care.


  • First Letter "M" in the Center: Placing the initial letter of the pharmacy's name in the center adds a personalized touch to the logo. It helps in brand recognition and makes the logo unique to this particular pharmacy.

Color


  • CF0954: This pinkish-red shade is associated with vitality, energy, and action. In a medical context, it can represent health, vibrancy, and the proactive nature of seeking medical assistance.


  • 0077B6: This blue shade is calming and trustworthy. In healthcare, blue symbolizes professionalism, trustworthiness, and reliability. It conveys calmness and stability, essential in healthcare services.
