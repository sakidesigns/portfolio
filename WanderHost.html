Project

WanderHost

The Backpacker's Free Accommodation Exchange App

Overview

Wander Host is a mobile app connecting backpackers with hosts offering free accommodation in exchange for volunteering. Travelers search for hosts based on location and volunteering opportunities, create profiles, communicate, and coordinate stays. The app fosters cultural exchange, sustainable travel, and meaningful connections between travelers and local communities.

The Problem Statement


  • Gap in understanding traveler needs for volunteering stays.

  • Poor search tools and limited visibility for finding hosts.

  • Communication hurdles and challenges in coordinating stays.

  • Neglected cultural exchange and sustainability focus.

  • Demand for improved UX in search and booking experiences.

The Solution Statement


  • Enhanced user research to understand travelers' needs.

  • Improved search tools for finding hosts.

  • Streamlined communication between travelers and hosts.

  • Incorporate cultural exchange and sustainability.

  • User-centered design for a seamless booking experience.

Empathize

I conducted qualitative research by interviewing five individuals aged between early 20s and 30s, all engaged in international trips for both work and leisure purposes. The interviews were designed to gather insights from potential users from various countries, including the United States, Japan, and Malaysia. This research aimed to understand their travel behaviors, preferences, and pain points to inform the design of a user-centered experience.




Intro
Questions:


  1. What does your typical weekday look like, especially regarding your travel plans or volunteering activities?

  2. When do you usually use the internet, and how does it relate to your travel or volunteering interests?

  3. Which apps and websites do you frequently use for travel bookings or finding volunteering opportunities?

  4. Can you share insights about your role in your company and how it may influence your travel or volunteering schedule?

Problem Identification
Questions:


  1. How do you view the relationship between travel experiences and volunteering opportunities?

  2. What methods do you currently use to plan your trips or find volunteering projects?

  3. How much time do you usually spend researching or organizing your travel and volunteering arrangements?

  4. Could you describe a recent experience related to your travel plans or volunteering efforts?

Behavioral
Questions:


  1. How often do you eat out during your travels or volunteering trips?

  2. What type of media do you consume regularly, and does it influence your travel decisions?

  3. How do you typically get around when traveling or volunteering (car, public transport, walking)?

  4. What technological devices do you use frequently while traveling or volunteering?

Based on the user interview, the insights were categorized into five categories: Personal Growth, Connectivity, Lifestyle, and Challenges.

In this storyboard, we follow Sarah as she navigates through our app, exploring its features and functionalities. Through Sarah's journey, we gain valuable insights into her motivations, interactions, and overall user experience, helping us refine and optimize the app for a seamless and delightful user journey.


1. Japan Adventure


Sarah, an adventurous traveler, plans her trip to Japan and searches for accommodation options.
She discovers the app offering free stays at hostels in exchange for volunteering and decides to explore further.


2. Creating a Profile


Sarah creates a detailed profile on the app, highlighting her skills in writing, photography, and environmental conservation. She uploads photos of her previous volunteer experiences and adds a description of her expectations and interests.


3. Finding the Right Host


Sarah uses the app's search filters to find hosts in Japan who are looking for volunteers with her skills. She reads reviews and ratings from other travelers to ensure she finds a host and place that matches her expectations.


4. Connecting with the Host


Sarah receives a message from a host in Kyoto interested in her skills and availability. She exchanges messages with the host, discussing volunteer tasks, accommodation details, and cultural experiences.


5. Arriving in Kyoto


Sarah arrives in Kyoto and is warmly welcomed by her host at the hostel. She settles into her accommodations and starts volunteering, helping with local community projects and cultural activities.


6. Cultural Immersion


Sarah immerses herself in Japanese culture, participating in tea ceremonies, visiting temples, and exploring the city. She expresses gratitude to her host for the enriching experience, new friendships, and meaningful cultural exchange during her stay in Japan.

Ideate

In the ideate phase, Wander Host emerges as a promising platform fostering cultural exchange and deep connections between travelers and local communities through volunteering opportunities. Its emphasis on sustainability by offering free accommodation in exchange for volunteer work is commendable.

I like


  • I like that Wander Host facilitates cultural exchange and meaningful connections between travelers and local communities.


  • I like the flexibility Wander Host offers travelers to search for hosts based on location and volunteering opportunities.


  • I like how Wander Host encourages sustainable travel practices through volunteering in exchange for free accommodation.

I wish


  • I wish Wander Host had more options for travelers to personalize their volunteering experiences based on their skills and interests.


  • I wish there were more resources or guides on cultural etiquette and local customs available on Wander Host for travelers.


  • I wish there was a feature on Wander Host that allowed travelers to leave reviews or ratings for their volunteering experiences to help others make informed choices.

What if


  • What if Wander Host partnered with local NGOs or organizations to offer a wider range of volunteering opportunities?


  • What if Wander Host integrated language learning tools or cultural workshops to enhance the cultural exchange experience?


  • What if Wander Host introduced a feature where travelers could share their travel itineraries or collaborate on volunteering projects with other travelers?

I assessed the feasibility and complexity of implementing each feature, taking into account development resources, time constraints, and technical requirements. This evaluation helped prioritize features that were both impactful and feasible within the project scope.

User Test

User testing involved 4 participants interacting with the Wander Host app prototype. Tasks like searching for hosts, creating profiles, and messaging were assessed. Success rates were calculated for each task, identifying areas needing improvement. This iterative process ensures the app meets user needs effectively.

During user testing, some users found it difficult to browse hosts due to a lack of suggestions and categorized hosts on the landing page. Additionally, communicating with hosts was challenging for some users, particularly in navigating the inbox. These insights highlight areas for improvement in the app's landing page design and messaging functionality.

Here are some key elements from user quotes during the usability test, categorized into content issues, navigation issues, and user interface issues.

Content Issues


  • “The Contact Host button leading to the Inbox is confusing.”


  • “NGO is not explained. What is NGO?”


  • "I find the suggested hosts in the host list helpful. I like this feature."


  • "Can you provide more details on how users can take part in study abroad programs or volunteer opportunities while they're still in school?"


Navigation Issues


  • “The registration process was smooth, but I found it a bit confusing to add interests to my profile.”


  • “The Contact Host button leading to the Inbox is confusing.”


User Interface Issues


  • “The inbox list lacks avatar pictures, making it hard to find specific messages.”


  • “It’s not always clear how to get back to the main menu.”
