My Journey into UX Design

Inspiration from Quality Management Administration





My journey into UX design was significantly influenced by my role as a Quality Management Administrator and interpreter in the automotive industry. I worked in a factory preparing to start production, where I witnessed firsthand how user experience can make or break a system.

Around the time I was hired, the company had invested in a document control system to replace all paper documents and streamline the audit process. However, many employees found it difficult to use, and some eventually stopped using it altogether. I investigated the reasons behind this, conducting interviews and observing workers on the floor. My ability to communicate with both Japanese and American workers gave me valuable insights into how people interact with software.

Through my investigation, I discovered that certain UX components of the software were discouraging employees from using it. The software was not designed to match the workflow of workers in different departments and roles. They frequently struggled with the software, either due to its complexity or poor design, leading to outdated documents being used and essential updates being missed.

This experience highlighted the critical importance of user-centered design. I realized that even the most advanced systems can fail if they do not consider the user's needs and behavior. This revelation ignited my passion for UX design, driving me to create solutions that are not only efficient but also intuitive and accessible.
